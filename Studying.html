Studying

The guide to Dutchness

Student life can be stressful, but Amsterdam offers great amenities. For example, the city features a wide variety of urban jungle cafés. Urban in the sense that they have fantastic coffees, resourceful menus and free Wi-Fi, and jungly in the sense that they have a buzzing yet tranquil atmosphere. This chapter lists them below, but also guides you through other essentials about studying in Amsterdam.

Course Materials

Textbooks can be purchased in specialised bookshops, but you can also get most textbooks in online bookshops like bol.com. Here you can find new and second-hand copies. Bol.com also a good place for selling textbooks that you no longer need. Athenaeum Boekhandel has bookshops in the city centre and near various university buildings. You can purchase books via Athenaeum (Dutch only). Scheltema on Rokin, a huge bookshop in the centre, also has a great choice and a good website, Scheltema. Studystore has bookshops in or near various universities in Amsterdam. They also offer books online. For more information, go to www.studystore.nl/ en. You can also visit Studystore to sell textbooks you no longer use.

Photocopying

For photocopying, you can use the machines at your faculty or those in libraries, shops and supermarkets. At commercial copy centres you can also have your papers printed and bound. One of these is Lowie Kopie copy centres, which has four shops where you can print, bind, copy and make your own business cards. You can also send them your work online and pick it up later. Visit Lowiekopie to find the nearest Lowie Kopie Copy Centre.

Dutch language courses

If you have obtained the necessary preparatory diplomas in your country of origin and are planning to enrol in a Dutch course, you will need a certificate stating that you have sufficient Dutch language skills. Students in exchange programmes do not need this certificate, but of course some knowledge of the Dutch language makes it easier to enjoy your stay. Your university can tell you about Dutch language courses. Sometimes the institutions offer Dutch language courses. You can contact their websites for information. You can also start a Dutch language course on the internet. Go to Duolingo and click on ‘Dutch’. It is a free resource with lessons varying from beginner’s tips to fun vocabulary. ISN Amsterdam offers 6 and 12 week courses where you will learn how to introduce yourself, how to ask for directions, and lots more. The 6 week course costs only €60, and the 12 week course costs €150, including a book called ‘Nederlands in Gang’. For more information, check their website.

University libraries

All educational institutes have their own libraries, where you can borrow books using your student ID card or library card. Most libraries also have reading rooms, where you can study or read the books you are not allowed to take out

Public libraries

The largest public library is the one at the Oosterdokseiland, near Centraal Station: the Centrale OBA or simply OBA in brief. OBA stands for Openbare Bibliotheek Amsterdam (Amsterdam Public Library). This library has a wide selection of foreign newspapers and is a good place to start if you want to learn more about Amsterdam. To get a library card you need to bring valid identifi cation papers and a document stating your current address (a recent telephone bill or bank statement will do). You can also fi ll in the form on their English website to enrol OBA. The OBA organises various events, many with free entry. These include lunch concerts in cooperation with the Conservatorium, city walks and bike rides, talks and reading events

Benji’

A nice study place located in the east close to HvA. It is also suitable for just grabbing breakfast or have a drink in the evening. They have plants hanging from the ceiling and the huge windows from fl oor to ceiling add a nice vibe to it. Pillows across the windowsills make it a perfect place to relax. And the industrial design and interior make this a perfect urban Amsterdam café. Wibautstraat 196

CT Coffee&Coconuts

The café is located in De Pijp in a former cinema of the 1920s called Ceintuur Theater. It is a large café with several fl oors, so there is always a place for studying. The interior is a very warm, funky, wooden feel mixed with rather industrial steel racks and brick walls. A perfect place if you want to get inspired. Ceintuurbaan 282-284

Volkshotel

Even if you don’t come to Volkshotel to study, this is a place you have to go to. This place is located in the east and they have a cool interior design with old objects and a mix of industrial elements, colours and plants. It is a real experience and a must-see. Volkshotel is a perfect place for studying. You can work in the café, at the workspaces with the big tables or in the work cabins that are available. Wibautstraat 150

Tosti Creative

Tosti is Dutch for a grilled sandwich, topped with either ham, cheese, or both, which you dip in ketchup before you take a bite. You can get them almost anywhere, from sports clubs to fancy bistros. Tosti creative has taken them to a whole new level with extra toppings of mushrooms, goats cheese, chicken or warm apple. Prices are reasonable and it has a creative vibe. Not only a great place to eat, but also a wonderful study spot - you can find them in De Pijp. Van Woustraat 2

De Stadskantine

De Stadskantine is located in De Pijp. This is the place to go if you want to study and have a nice meal for a cheap price. They have simple food for breakfast, lunch and dinner. It can be expensive to go out for dinner in Amsterdam, but here you can get a nice, healthy meal for under €10. The dinner menu contains a choice of fish, veggie or meat. This place is very cool and urban and has a laid back atmosphere. Van Woustraat 120

Hortus Botanicus

Amsterdam’s Hortus Botanicus contains over 6,000 different indigenous and non-native trees and plants. In addition to outdoor gardens, they have a café, and a ‘hothouse’ that emulates three tropical environments, and hundreds of majestic butterfl ies can be found inside the Botanicus’ Butterfl y Greenhouse. Entry is €9 for an adult, or €5 with a student card

NEMO

Did you know that museum NEMO has a green roof? Here you can enjoy beachy vibes and a magnifi cent view. The entree is €16.50 for adults or €8.25 with a student card. You can take your own picnic, so what are you waiting for?

Klein Dantzig

Klein Dantzig is a beautiful park with different and unique allotments in Amsterdam East. Interested in beekeeping? Then this is the place to go. You can find this spot in the tranquil green oasis called Frankendael Par

‘t Nieuwe Diep

You will find this distillery in a leafy green corner of Flevopark, surrounded by water, fruit trees and herb gardens. It is hidden in the middle of the park; you can enjoy a few beers or a home-made jenever with some snacks and a peaceful view

De Hoftuin

De Hoftuin is a quiet spot hidden behind the Hermitage museum. This garden café is the place to go for high tea, lunch or a drink. The restaurant uses its own herbs and veggies for the dishes. After your meal you can take a look at the flowers and sculptures in the gardens. Entry is free
