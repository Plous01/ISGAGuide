Arts & Culture

The guide to Dutchness

ARTS&CULTURE

The Dutch are keen to foster any type of art: from the Old Masters of the 17th Century, to Dutch Design, to the export of television formats like Big Brother or The Voice–culture in the Netherlands seems to sprout as lushly as vegetation in a jungle. Read this chapter to equip yourself with the basic need-to-knows on arts and culture.

Discount cards

Some theatres and concert halls give discounts to anyone with a student card. Other discount options are buying the Museum Card (Museumkaart) or the Cultural Youth Passport (Cultureel Jongeren Paspoort, CJP). Make sure you get at least one the following discount cards!

  • The museumcard - The Museumkaart is a worthwhile investment for frequent museum visitors. With this card you will get free entry or a considerable reduction for up to 400 Dutch museums for one year. If you are aged eighteen or under, it costs €32.95 (plus a one-time administration fee of €4.95). If you are 19 or over, the card costs €59.90 (plus a one-time administration fee of €4.95). You can order the card on the website, which also contains a list of participating museums. Take a look at www.museumkaart.nl (Dutch only) for more information
  • CJP pass - If you are aged 29 or under you may want to consider buying a CJP (Cultureel Jongeren Paspoort) pass, which gives you discounts on movies, plays, magazine subscriptions and CDs. A CJP pass is yours for only €17.50. You can buy one at the Amsterdam Uitburo, Tourist Information Offices and on the CJP website. Check the CJP website for available discounts: CJP
  • ESN Card - For €5 you can buy an ESN Card at the office of your ESN section which gives you a discount at several museums, bars and shops in Amsterdam. You can use this card all over Europe and enjoy the great deals that are linked to it. For more information check www.isn-amsterdam.nl/esn-card-deals and www.esncard.org. The main place to buy your tickets is the Amsterdam Uitburo. They also have The Last Minute Ticket shop, which gives you 25 to 75 percent discount if you buy your tickets on the day of the show (you have to pay €2 service costs). This only applies to selected performances. The Last Minute starts every day at 10.00 am with a maximum of two tickets per transaction. A great deal is the I Amsterdam city card. There is the option to purchase a 24, 48, 72 or 96 hour card. Museums

Amsterdam has many great museums. The jewel in the crown is the Rijksmuseum but there are many more that are worth a visit. Also, every year on the first Saturday in November, Amsterdam opens its museums during Museum Night (Museumnacht). This one-night event is very special and includes all kinds of activities in several participating museums, from concerts to visits by tattoo artists. In 2017 Museum Night will take place on 4 November. For tickets and information go to Amsterdam Uitburo or www.n8.nl (Dutch only).

The Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum is the largest and best-known museum in the country. The building is a national monument designed by the architect Cuijpers. It houses an internationally renowned collection of paintings from the Dutch Golden Age, with Rembrandt’s Night Watch as the centrepiece. The Rijksmuseum also has Delftware, drawings and Asian art. Did you know that you can cycle underneath the museum via a bicycle tunnel? Visit the website for more details: www.rijksmuseum.nl. Museumstraat 1

The Stedelijk Museum

The Stedelijk Museum focuses on modern contemporary art. It has a famous collection of paintings by Picasso, Mondriaan and Warhol, sculptures, photographs and graphic designs. You can’t miss the museum’s new wing. It has not been nicknamed ‘the bathtub’ for nothing. Visit the website for more details: www.stedelijk.nl. Museumplein 10

The Van Gogh Museum

The Van Gogh Museum boasts the largest collection of paintings in the world by the famous Dutch painter Vincent van Gogh. The museum also hosts temporary exhibitions featuring 19th century art and related subjects. Their audio tour is worthwhile. Visit the website for more details: www.vangoghmuseum.nl Museumplein 6

The Hermitage Amsterdam

In 2009 the world-famous Hermitage Museum in Saint Petersburg opened an annexe in Amsterdam. Located in a former nursing home, it hosts temporary exhibitions of art from the enormous collection of the Saint Petersburg Museum. It also has an impressive gallery of huge group portraits of the Golden Age. Visit the website for more details: www.hermitage.nl Amstel 51

The Heineken Experienc

Heineken beer has been brewed in Amsterdam since 1863, making it a classic Dutch beer brand. The museum is housed in the old Heineken brewery and has been transformed into a real ‘experience’. The Heineken Experience guides you through all aspects of the brand. Here you can literally see, hear, feel, smell and of course taste, the beer. On the tour you will hear all about the distinctive Heineken taste and you can also take a look inside the old brewery. Visit the website for more details: www.heineken.com Stadhouderskade 78

The Dutch Maritime Museum

The collection at the Dutch Maritime Museum (Het Scheepvaartmuseum) is housed in the former naval depot, built in 1656. The museum owns a replica of the Amsterdam, one of the trading ships of the Dutch East India Company (VOC). Visit the website for more details: www.hetscheepvaartmuseum.nl Kattenburgerplein 1

The Amsterdam Museum

The Amsterdam Museum is housed in the Burgerweeshuis. This former orphanage dates back to the 17th century and traces Amsterdam’s history through the ages. Visit the website for more details: www.amsterdammuseum.nl Kalverstraat 92

Theatres

The theatres in Amsterdam offer plays to suit everyone’s taste. The largest and best-known are mentioned below. The Amsterdam Uitburo provides information and programmes

Koninklijk Theater Carré

This theatre was named after circus manager Oscar Carré, who built the theatre on the Amstel River front. Carré hosts the larger shows such as musicals, cabaret and ballet. For more information visit their website: www.carre.nl Amstel 115-125

Stadsschouwburg

The city theatre at Leidseplein is the direct descendant of Amsterdam’s first theatre, built in 1617. The theatre boasts an extensive collection of portraits of famous Dutch actors and actresses. Stadsschouwburg is also home to a café-restaurant. In summer the outdoor area is a wonderful place to do some studying. Leidseplein 26

The Muziektheater

The Muziektheater shares its building with the town hall of Amsterdam. The name of the building is Stopera. At the Muziektheater you can see the Dutch Opera and the Dutch National Ballet, as well as other performances by guest companies. It is nice to know that students who are at the night box office (avondkassa) have a good chance to get a cheaper ticket. These are tickets, for example, that have not been collected by patrons. Ask for a number at the box office and about fifteen minutes before the show starts you will know if there is a ticket for you. Amstel 3

DeLaMar Theater

The newest theatre in the city centre of Amsterdam is the DeLaMar Theater, situated near Leidseplein. It offers a varied programme for a varied audience. Cabaret, musicals and international plays can be seen in this theatre. It is a contemporary theatre with two halls, a Grand Café and several foyers. Marnixstraat 402

Pathé

You can have a good time at one of the four Pathé cinemas; Pathé runs four big cinema complexes in Amsterdam, the most impressive being the Pathé Arena, which also hosts an IMAX theatre and shows movies on a huge and impressive screen. Pathé Tuschinski is situated in a magnificent Art Deco building. A lot of Dutch movies have been filmed in this building. Pathé cinemas screen mostly Hollywood blockbusters, but also some art house movies (PAC), Turkish and Bollywood movies and Gay Classics. On Tuesday evenings Pathé offers special sneak previews. Film reviews and programmes can be found in Amsterdam magazines, cinema foyers and on www.filmladder.nl

The Movies

This cinema is the oldest movie theatre in Amsterdam. Here you can find a wide list with everything from commercial movies to interesting foreign movies. Haarlemmerdijk 161

Kriterion

Kriterion was created in 1945 to employ students in order to make it possible for them to afford their studies. Nowadays, the cinema is still attractive to students, as it offers quality movies and interesting documentaries. Roetersstraat 170

Studio/K

This place is more than just a cinema. It is also a restaurant, a club, a gallery and a concert venue run by students. The focus is on high quality movies from other places than Hollywood. Timorplein 62

Cinecentre

This is the best place for the latest high quality art movies, especially from the French and Spanish speaking regions. Lijnbaansgracht 236

Rialto

This cinema specialises in showing unique art movies from both Europe and the rest of the world. Rialto is also the host of the annual festival World Cinema Amsterdam. Ceintuurbaan 338

Festivals

Various theatre and film festivals are organised in and around Amsterdam

Holland Festival

The world-famous Holland Festival is a multi-disciplinary and international festival for the dramatic arts. Students can get a considerable discount on performances. See for more information: www.hollandfestival.nl

The International Documentary Film Festival Amsterdam (IDFA)

The International Documentary Film Festival Amsterdam is a festival known worldwide that celebrates and stimulates national and international creative documentaries. During IDFA more than 250 documentaries are shown. IDFA is from 16 November to 27 November 2017. See for more information: www.idfa.nl

The International Film Festival Rotterdam

The International Film Festival Rotterdam (IFFR) is held annually in various cinemas in Rotterdam at the end of January. The next film festival is from 25 January to 5 February 2018. The focus is on independent and experimental films by emerging talents and established authors. See for more information: www.filmfestivalrotterdam.com

The Amsterdam Gay Pride

- The Amsterdam Gay Pride event is held from 3 to 5 August 2018. This festival allows LGBT+ people to show that they are proud to be who they are. They celebrate this with many festivals and parties. The biggest is the Canal Parade, where lots of colourful boats sail through the canals. See for more information: www.amsterdamgaypride.org

Uitmark

In the weekend of 25 August 2017 the fortieth edition of the Uitmarkt will take place. This marks the start of the new cultural season. You can attend various open-air concerts. There is music, drama and comedy and there are markets and exhibitions you can enjoy for free.

The Westergasfabriek

These buildings were erected in 1883. In the past it supplied gas for the city’s street lighting, but the complex has now been converted into a trendy cultural park where you can go for dinner, drinks, partying, a play or a movie. The gasworks and the surrounding area have been beautifully restored and now house many creative and cultural tenants. There are thirteen cafés and the park hosts many events every year

Brown cafés (Bruine cafés)
